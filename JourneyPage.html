My Journey

Growing Up Nigerian

I was born and bred in the oil rich city of Warri, Delta State, Nigeria. I have 5 brothers and two sisters. I'm the 5th child, and everyone's favorite. Growing up in Nigeria was tough! I learned about diligence and hardwork from a very tender age. Dad was a marine engineer and mom owned a grocery store. My father use to say "Education is the best legacy you can give to a child" wise man! I went to school when my folks could afford my tuition because in my part of the world there is no such thing as "free eductaion". Unfortunately, I dropped out of high school after my dad lost his job and couldn't afford my tuition. Nonetheless, I self-studied and earned my GED after a few tries. I graduated with honors from Ambrose Alli University in 2012 where I was awarded a BSc in Economics.

The Great Transition

My insatiable thirst for knowledge motivated me to relocate to the U.S to further my education in 2014. When I arrived in New York, I instantly knew that I was destined to be here. The euphoria of arriving in a new country was short lived because I was on a mission to be great. I decided to enlist in the military in an attempt to contribute to this nation that has transformed the lives of so many immigrants like myself. I received tuition assistance and other amazing benefits while serving as an active duty Soldier. I graduated with honors from Murray State University in 2020. I was awarded an MBA with a concentration in healthcare administration. God is good!

Go Home

Creating My Future

As I reminisce on my journey thus far, I am excited about what God has in store for me. I left active duty military in 2021 after the untimely demise of my lovely spouse to pursue a career in IT. I work for an IT company as a Software Development Engineer in Test (SDET). I am passionate about ensuring that our end-users have the best user experience hence my decision to become a full stack software developer so I can be closer to the software achitecture. Finally, I want to use this medium to thank God, my family and my late spouse (melaneice) for having my back all these years. The marathon continues!
